What is this

DATA BROKERS ARE THE INFRASTRUCTURE OF MODERN SURVEILLANCE.

Data brokers are companies that collect personal information — from public records, app location data, social media, and purchase history — and sell it to anyone who pays. No warrant. No oversight. No notification to you.

ICE and other government agencies have spent millions purchasing this data to locate, track, and arrest immigrants — sidestepping constitutional protections that would normally require judicial approval.

Georgetown Law's Center on Privacy and Technology calls it the "American Dragnet": a mass surveillance system built from commercial data, targeting immigrant communities.

The good news: US privacy law gives you the right to opt out of data broker databases. It's a slow, tedious process — each broker has its own form, its own verification step, its own delay.

This tool automates it. Enter your information once. We walk you through all 107 databases — auto-filling what we can, giving you step-by-step instructions for the rest.

Install the Chrome extension for full automation: the extension opens each opt-out tab in the background, fills the form, and submits — completely without you needing to interact.

Sources: Georgetown Law Center on Privacy and Technology, "American Dragnet" (2022); Brennan Center for Justice; ACLU; Electronic Frontier Foundation.
